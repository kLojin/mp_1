Discover Salcedo:

A Journey Through Natural Beauty and Rich History

Nestled in the heart of Eastern Samar, Salcedo invites you to explore a world of breathtaking natural wonders and immerse yourself in a rich tapestry of history and culture. Our municipality is a hidden gem, waiting to be discovered by intrepid travelers and curious souls.

Historical

BACKGROUND

Brief History

  • Initially, Salcedo was named Naga because of the abundance of growing Narra trees in the place.
  • Salcedo was situated in the midst of a wide fertile plain suited for agriculture with a forest area in the north-western hills which was then and presently an excellent source of sparkling cold water brought down by a river that bisects the town.
  • The town was named after a conqueror of Luzon, Juan Salcedo, by virtue of a religious decree of a then Bishop of Cebu.
  • The Municipality of Salcedo’s Foundation Day was enacted into Municipal Ordinance No. 01-04, series of 2001 on the 5th of December of every year.
  • Foundation Day is declared as an official local holiday for all government offices including schools within the municipality’s jurisdiction. This municipal ordinance was approved on August 08, 2001.

Salcedo Profile

  • It is a fifth class municipality located in the province of Eastern Samar. It is situated at coordinates 11 14’ north latitude and 125 34’ to 125 42’ east longitude. The tertritory is bounded to the north by the Matarinao Bay, to the west by Quinapondan, to south-east by Mercedes, to the south by the Leyte Gulf and the Pacific Ocean to the east. It is about 92 kilometers southeast of Borongan, the provincial capital, and 17 kilometers northwest of Guiuan, a growth center town in the south of the province. Via south Samar Coastal Road, the municiapality is 137 kilometers from Tacloban City, the capital region.
  • It is composed of forty one (41) barangays; thirteen (13) of which are poblacion barangays (twenty four [24] are coastal barangays, two [2] island barangays and fifteen [15] inland barangays). The Municipality has a total area of 113.68 square kilometers which comprises the 2.3 % of the provincial area.
